Prioritize hydration, movement, rest, and baggage continuity. Use layover time conservatively. Do not burn energy trying to over-tour during a connection.

Housing Evaluation Checklist
Location: Walking distance to food, 7-Eleven, pharmacy, beach, grocery, and transport.
Livability: Noise, sunlight, balcony usability, air conditioning, mattress quality, work space, and storage.
Building: Elevator, security, gym, pool, maintenance, cleanliness, pest control, trash handling.
Utilities: Internet speed, water pressure, power reliability, AC cost, laundry access.
Lease fit: Monthly rent, deposit, lease term, utilities, maintenance responsibility, exit terms.
Vehicle need: Can daily life work without owning a car? If not, note exact reason.
Medical / Health Recon
Hospital access: Identify nearest reliable hospital and realistic travel time from target housing areas.
Pharmacy: Confirm access to routine medications, English support, and refill practicality.
Emergency: Know route, transport option, and whether emergency help is easy to activate.
Chronic care: Assess sleep apnea/CPAP supplies, kidney-safe medication access, anticoagulant support, and lab testing access.
Heat tolerance: Track symptoms during walks, meals, and transfers. Note shade, rest points, and hydration access.
Fitness: Identify hotel gym limits and nearest serious gym for resistance training.
Daily-Life Recon
Morning: Beach walk, coffee, breakfast, heat level, traffic, noise.
Midday: Grocery/pharmacy errands, transportation stress, shade, hydration, rest options.
Evening: Food choices, safety feel, lighting, noise, walkability, market access.
Cost tracking: Record meals, transport, groceries, laundry, tips, coffee, and incidental spending.
Social fit: Expat presence, local friendliness, language friction, church/community potential.
Ordinary Tuesday test: Would this location still work when nothing exciting is happening?
