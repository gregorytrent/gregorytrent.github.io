Pre-trip Checklist
Need To Know
Thai people generally value calmness, patience, and respectful interactions. Raising your voice rarely improves outcomes.
Hello: Sawasdee khrap
Thank you: Khop khun khrap
Excuse me / Sorry: Khor thot khrap
Tourist Police: 1155
Emergency Medical Services: 1669
Most malls, restaurants, and convenience stores accept credit cards, but cash remains useful for local markets, taxis, and small vendors.
Download Grab before arrival. Grab will likely become your primary transportation tool.
7-Eleven stores are everywhere and are often the fastest place for snacks, drinks, SIM top-ups, medicine, and basic necessities.
The hottest part of the day is typically between noon and 4 PM. Schedule heavy recon activities early morning whenever possible.
Always carry water. Thailand’s heat and humidity can dehydrate visitors much faster than expected.
Crosswalks do not guarantee vehicles will stop. Always verify traffic before crossing.
Motorbikes are everywhere. Check both directions even on one-way streets.
When evaluating neighborhoods, revisit them during morning, afternoon, evening, and weekend periods before making judgments.
Do not sign a long-term lease during the recon trip. Use the trip to narrow choices rather than make permanent commitments.
Pay attention to grocery access, pharmacy access, hospital access, and daily convenience more than beach views or tourism appeal.
Ask yourself: Could I comfortably live an ordinary Tuesday here?
Hua Hin generally offers a calmer lifestyle, while Pattaya offers more services and activity.
Always compare housing locations based on travel time rather than distance.
Use this trip to identify deal-breakers as much as advantages.
If a location feels significantly different at night than during the day, document it immediately in your notes.
The goal of this recon is not to fall in love with Thailand. The goal is to determine whether daily life in Thailand aligns with your actual lifestyle needs.
