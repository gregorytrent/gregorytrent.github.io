MANDATORY LONG-FLIGHT MEDICAL PROTOCOL (ALL FLIGHTS OVER 6 HOURS)
Clot Prevention: Compression socks required, aggressive hydration, and no prolonged sitting.
Doctor Authorization: Written clearance and medication guidance required prior to travel.
Movement Schedule: Stand or walk every 45–60 minutes. Pre-select aisle seating whenever possible.
Fatigue Rule: No aggressive recon activity immediately after long-haul arrival days.
Thailand Relocation Strategy
Primary objective is a structured, reality-based relocation evaluation rather than tourism. The recon phase is designed to determine sustainable daily living compatibility, healthcare practicality, transportation stress levels, housing quality, grocery access, beach accessibility, long-term comfort, and operational simplicity.
Primary target area: Hua Hin because of calmer atmosphere, beach accessibility, lower chaos, and stronger retirement/lifestyle alignment.
Secondary comparison area: Pattaya/Jomtien to evaluate convenience, healthcare concentration, transportation practicality, and housing value.
Bangkok functions primarily as a transit and infrastructure node rather than a preferred long-term residential target.

Operational Logic
Land at BKK → transfer directly to Hua Hin.
Use Hua Hin for primary housing, lifestyle, and operational recon.
Move to Pattaya/Jomtien afterward for comparative analysis rather than primary commitment.
Use Bangkok only for airport connectivity and transit flexibility.
Phased Move Structure

Phase 1 — Recon

Validate actual livability, transportation stress, medical practicality, neighborhood compatibility, and daily operational comfort before making commitments.

Phase 2 — Trial Living

Extended temporary stay structure while maintaining fallback flexibility and evaluating sustainability beyond vacation conditions.

Phase 3 — Long-Term Positioning

Transition toward stable long-term living structure after confirming healthcare systems, housing stability, budgeting, and overall life alignment.

Phase 4 — Optimization

Refine transportation systems, community integration, cost structure, medical logistics, and operational simplicity for sustainable long-term execution.

Core Decision Filters
Does this location support peace rather than constant friction?
Can ordinary daily life operate efficiently without exhaustion?
Is healthcare realistically accessible?
Would this still feel sustainable after novelty disappears?
Does this environment support long-term stability rather than temporary excitement?
Reality Rules
Do not sign leases immediately after first impressions.
Revisit finalist neighborhoods at multiple times of day.
Test grocery access, pharmacy access, and traffic patterns directly.
Evaluate transportation fatigue honestly.
Comfort and sustainability matter more than social-media aesthetics.
