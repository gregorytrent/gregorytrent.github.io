Why the U.S. School Path Is Still Best

If Maurice wants to live in Thailand while going to school, the strongest plan is to keep his main college in the United States and use a Thai school to support his visa. Thailand has an education visa, called the ED visa, that allows a student to stay in the country while enrolled in an approved school. This works well for legal stay and daily structure, but it is not the strongest way to protect a college degree.

A U.S. college keeps Maurice on a path toward a degree that is widely recognized and easier to use later for jobs, transfer, or graduate school. The biggest risk in switching the main degree path to Thailand is credit loss. Thai schools may accept U.S. credits, but that process is case by case. In a better case, Maurice may lose about 0% to 25% of credits if the classes line up well. In a more common middle case, he could lose about 25% to 50%. In a worse case, especially if the classes do not match the Thai program well, he could lose more than 50% of usable progress and be forced to repeat classes.

Cost is where Thailand becomes attractive. A Thai language program tied to an ED visa is usually much cheaper than a full U.S. degree path. The Thai school can handle the visa and provide structure. The U.S. school can remain the real degree path. In simple terms, the Thai school supports where Maurice lives now. The U.S. school supports where he is going later.

Best model: Thai school = legal stay + structure. U.S. school = degree protection + future flexibility.
